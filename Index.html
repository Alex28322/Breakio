Frequently asked questions

Here are answers to some of the most common things people ask us. Whether you're new to the platform or just curious about how things work, this section is here to help you feel informed and supported.

Is this platform a replacement for therapy?

No, this platform is not a replacement for professional therapy or mental health treatment. Our tools are designed to support emotional awareness, daily reflection, and personal growth—but they are not intended to diagnose or treat mental health conditions. If you're experiencing serious emotional distress, we encourage you to seek support from a licensed mental health professional.

How is my personal data and emotional input protected?

Your privacy and safety are a top priority. All emotional check-ins and personal information are stored securely using encryption and industry-standard protection methods. We do not sell, share, or expose your data to third parties, and you are always in control of what you choose to share or delete.

Can I use the platform anonymously?

Yes, you can. We understand that emotional reflection is deeply personal, and that’s why we allow you to use the platform without revealing your identity. You can check in, write, and track your emotions without creating a public profile or sharing personal details.

How often should I check in with my emotions?

There’s no right or wrong frequency—it depends on your needs. Some users prefer to check in daily as part of a morning or evening routine, while others use it a few times a week or only during stressful moments. We encourage you to find a rhythm that feels natural and supportive for you.

Is the service free to use? Are there any paid features?

Yes, the core features of the platform are completely free to use. This includes emotional check-ins, journaling, and basic reflection tools. We may offer optional paid features in the future—such as advanced analytics, guided exercises, or personalized insights—but the main experience will always remain free.
